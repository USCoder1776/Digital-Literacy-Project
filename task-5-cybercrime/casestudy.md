# Phishing Attack Case Study

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick users into sharing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or personal data. It usually happens through fake emails, messages, or websites that appear legitimate.

In a typical phishing attack, the user receives an email that looks like it is from a trusted company. The email creates urgency, such as warning about account suspension or offering a limited-time opportunity. It contains a link that redirects to a fake website. When the user enters their credentials, the attacker gains access to their account.

Students are often targeted because they frequently check emails and may respond quickly without verification. The consequences include account hacking, financial loss, and misuse of personal data.

Phishing attacks are successful because they exploit human behavior such as trust, urgency, and lack of attention to detail.
